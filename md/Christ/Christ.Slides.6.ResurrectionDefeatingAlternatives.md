# The Resurrection: Defeating the Alternatives

---

## Competing Explanations

* Craig compares rival theories using historical inference
  * Establish key facts
  * Infer the best explanation
* Criteria (in the spirit of McCullagh):
  * Explanatory scope and power
  * Plausibility
  * Less ad hoc
  * Fit with background knowledge

---

## “Classic” Naturalistic Theories

* Conspiracy / stolen body
* Apparent death (swoon)
* Wrong tomb
* Displaced body

Each tends to fail by being too narrow, too fragile, or too implausible to explain the whole data set.

---

## Myth, Legend, and Hallucination Proposals

* Myth/legend proposals must reckon with early testimony and Jewish context
* Hallucination proposals struggle to explain:
  * The empty tomb
  * Group appearances
  * The content of the belief (“resurrection” vs “translation”)

---

## The Resurrection Hypothesis

* “God raised Jesus from the dead” explains:
  * Empty tomb
  * Appearances
  * Origin of belief
* Craig’s method links this to:
  * Broader theism (natural theology)
  * Jesus’ identity and claims

---

## Practical Implications

* The resurrection:
  * Vindicates Christ’s person and work
  * Enables present relationship with the living Lord
  * Grounds hope in death
  * Guarantees Christ’s return
