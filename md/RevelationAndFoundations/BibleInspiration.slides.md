  * General
  * Special

      * How did He inspire it?

## The Target of Inspiration is Scripture

![](InspirationPics/1Tim316.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

<p style="font-family: 'Gabriola', serif;">
    16 All **scripture** is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness,
</p><p style="text-align: right; margin-top: -1em;">    <em>2 Timothy 3:16 (NKJV)</em></p>

* Inspiration is a property of the text itself
* Not the authors
* Not of any translation (more below)

---

## Extent of Inspiration

![](InspirationPics/OrchestratedBeauty.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* Three properties of inspiration:
  1. Plenary
     1. All of scripture, all books, all verses
  2. Verbal
     1. Down to the individual words
  3. Confluent
     1. Authored by both God and man

---

## Plenary Inspiration

![](InspirationPics/BibleWithBeauty.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

<p style="font-family: 'Gabriola', serif;">

</p><p style="text-align: right; margin-top: -1em;">    <em>2 Timothy 3:16 (NKJV)</em></p>

* No part of the Bible can be set aside as uninspired

## Verbal Inspiration

![](InspirationPics/LightOnWords.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

<p style="font-family: 'Gabriola', serif;">
    16 Now to Abraham and his **Seed** were the promises made. He does not say, "And to **seeds**," as of many, but as of one, "And to your **Seed**," who is Christ.

* Inspiration extends to the individual specific words used

## Verbal Inspiration Types

![](InspirationPics/BibleDemonstrated.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* Propositional Revelation
  * The meaning of text
    * Snow is white == Schnee ist weiss
    * Language does not matter
* Sentential Revelation
  * The actual sentences and words of the text
  * The original Hebrew and Greek of the Bible
  * The original uses word meanings, word plays, idioms, rhymes
    * Translations cannot capture all of those at once
* Verbal Inspiration is Sentential

---

## Translation and Inspiration

![](InspirationPics/WorldWords.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* English translations are not the inspired Word of God
* They convey some part of word meanings, word plays, idioms, rhymes
* Different translations focus on different aspects
  * Word for word vs thought for thought
  * Gender-specific language vs gender-inclusive
  * Older vs purer manuscripts
* The real deal is to learn Hebrew and Greek and read the original
  * What the Bible teaches can be understood from English translations

---

## Manuscript Variants

![](InspirationPics/DifferentBooks.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* No original Bible manuscripts still exist
* Thousands of manuscript copies exist for OT and NT
* The copies have text differences or "variants"
* Most variants are minor (e.g., spelling, word order)
* Significant variants are rare and do not affect core doctrines
  * Mark 16:9-20 - Longer ending of Mark found in some manuscripts
  * 1 John 1:4 - "our joy" vs. "your joy"

<p style="font-family: 'Gabriola', serif;">
     4 And these things we write to you that **your** joy may be full.
</p><p style="text-align: right; margin-top: -2.5em;">    <em>1 John 1:4 (NKJV)</em></p>

---

## Recovering the Original

![](InspirationPics/ScrollDifferences.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* Variants exist due to copying errors over centuries
* Textual criticism helps reconstruct the original text with >99% accuracy
  * Only one occurrance differences, spelling mistakes
  * Majority vote on word order, prepositions ("a" vs "an"), ...
  * Skips: "they asked Jesus [to pray. So Jesus] prayed for them"
* Does not affect the Gospel, or even the conclusions we draw in this class

## Manuscript Families

|                     | Byzantine                                      | Alexandrian                                                                 |
|---------------------|------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Dated**           | 5th to 15th centuries                          | 2nd to 4th centuries                                                         |
| **First Discovered**| 16th century, Textus Receptus, common in Byzantine Empire| 18th century Codex Alexandrinus, Sinaiticus |
| **Key Features**    | More numerous, more complete, better quality & spelling | Older, shorter, multi-edited readings |

* Different translations may use different manuscript families

![](InspirationPics/OldLibrary.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

<p style="font-family: 'Gabriola', serif;">
    7 For there are three that bear witness in heaven: the Father, **the Word, and the Holy Spirit**; and these three are one. 8 And there are three that bear witness on earth: the Spirit, **the water, and the blood**; and these three agree as one.

* Alexandrian: no Comma Johanneum. NIV: the water and the blood

  * Different but not doctrine changing

* Older is better
  * Closer in time to original
  * Less chance for change

## Arguments for Byzantine Preference

![](InspirationPics/ScrollWithGoldLetters.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* Majority Text Argument
  * Most manuscripts: count and geography
  * => used most and preferred by early Christians
* Ecclesiastical Text Argument
  * Standard text used by the Greek-speaking church >1000 years
* Internal Evidence Argument
  * Exhibits smoother and more coherent readings, fewer corrections
* Doctrinal Integrity Argument
  * Preserves doctrinally significant readings
* Early copies destroyed through
  * Use, humidity, and Bible burnings

![](InspirationPics/LitmusTest.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

    36 But if any man thinks he is behaving improperly toward his virgin, if she is past the flower of youth, and thus it must be, let him do what he wishes. He does not sin; let them marry. 37 Nevertheless he who stands steadfast in his heart, having no necessity, but has power over his own will, and has so determined in his heart that he will keep his virgin, does well. 38 So then he who **gives her in marriage (γαμίσκω)** does well, but he who does not **give her in marriage (γαμίσκω)** does better.

## Theories of Inspiration

![](InspirationPics/ThinkingMan.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* Doctrine
  * Inspiration is plenary, verbal and **confluent**
    * Prophecy is dictation by God
    * Other passages are clearly human authored, yet still inspired

* Theories
  * **Dictation** theory: God tells the author what to write
    * Fails to explain human reporting and emotion expression
  * **Supervision** theory: Holy Spirit supervises the writing
    * Allows for human authorship and divine inspiration
    * OK, but how exactly? Mystery!?

## Supervision through Molinism

![](InspirationPics/GodLookingIn.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* God knows what **is** true: past, present and future
* God knows what **could** be true
* God knows what **would** be true, under any set of circumstances (middle knowledge)
* God knew what authors would freely write in any and all circumstances
* By placing authors in the circumstances with the desired writing outcome, God ensured the text he wanted
* Bible is God's message to us
* Verbal, plenary, and confluent inspiration

## Conclusion

![](InspirationPics/InspirationBook.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

* Inspiration is
  * Plenary - all of the Bible
  * Verbal - each word
  * Confluent - synergetic between God and man
* God's Word is revealed through human authors in Scripture
* The Bible is God's message to mankind
