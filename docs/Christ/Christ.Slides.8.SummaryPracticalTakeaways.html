# Doctrine of Christ Summary and Practical Takeaways --- ## The Doctrine of Christ (In One View) * The doctrine of Christ is about: * **Who Jesus is** (incarnation) * **What Jesus did** (death and resurrection) * **How salvation is received** (access) --- ## Four Pillars * Incarnation: fully God and fully man * Death: atonement addressing guilt, justice, and reconciliation * Resurrection: vindication, evidence, and future hope * Access: Christ as the saving doorway, and God’s justice and mercy toward all peoples --- ## A Useful Apologetic Pattern * Craig distinguishes two complementary routes: * Existential knowledge (encounter and discipleship) * Evidential knowledge (historical reasoning) * A common structure: * Establish key facts * Infer the best explanation --- ## Discipleship Implications * Worship * Jesus is worthy because of who he is * Hope * Resurrection reframes suffering and death * Holiness * Christ’s death is not only pardon, but transformation * Mission * If access is through Christ, love compels proclamation --- ## Navigation * [Return to Christ Overview](Christ.Slides.html) * Prev: [Access: Who Can Be Saved Through Christ?](Christ.Slides.7.AccessWhoCanBeSavedThroughChrist.html) * [Creekside Defenders Main Page](http://tinyurl.com/CreeksideDefenders) ---
