# Access: Who Can Be Saved Through Christ? --- ## The Question * If Christ’s work is the only atonement * Who can benefit from it? * The pastoral and apologetic pressure point: * What about those who never hear the gospel? --- ## The Biblical Case for Particularism * The New Testament presents salvation as through Christ * Not “many independent roads” * Craig contrasts particularism with religious pluralism * Pluralism often functions as the default view in modern culture --- ## Pluralism: Two Forms * Unsophisticated pluralism * “All religions are equally true” * Self-refuting because religions make mutually exclusive claims * Sophisticated pluralism * “All are equally false but equally effective” * Needs careful evaluation and evidence --- ## The Real Problem: The Unevangelized * Craig distinguishes sub-questions: * Would a loving God send anyone to hell? * Is eternal punishment unjust? * Is it unfair to judge those who never heard? * He argues God judges persons by response to general revelation * Christ’s atonement can apply without conscious knowledge of Christ --- ## The Logical Challenge * A common argument claims these cannot both be true: * God is all-powerful and all-loving * Some never hear and are lost * Craig targets the hidden assumptions * God cannot make someone freely choose * A world of universal salvation may not be feasible --- ## Middle Knowledge Solution (Outline) * God knows what every possible person would freely do * God can providentially order history so that: * Those who would respond are placed where they will hear * Those who never hear and are lost would not have believed anyway * This supports missions rather than undermining them * “Divine appointments” in evangelism --- ## Navigation * [Return to Christ Overview](Christ.Slides.html) * Prev: [The Resurrection: Defeating the Alternatives](Christ.Slides.6.ResurrectionDefeatingAlternatives.html) * Next: [Doctrine of Christ Summary and Practical Takeaways](Christ.Slides.8.SummaryPracticalTakeaways.html) * [Creekside Defenders Main Page](http://tinyurl.com/CreeksideDefenders) ---
