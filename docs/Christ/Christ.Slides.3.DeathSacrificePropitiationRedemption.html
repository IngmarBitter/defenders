# Christ’s Death: Sacrifice, Propitiation, and Redemption --- ## The Work of Christ * The doctrine of Christ includes: * Person (incarnation) * Work (atonement, resurrection, salvation applied) * Christ’s death is described with many biblical images * Craig treats these as complementary motifs, not competing “one-verse” theories --- ## Sacrifice * Atonement is often expressed in sacrificial categories * Temple, priesthood, blood, offering * Two closely related ideas: * **Expiation:** dealing with sin and guilt * **Propitiation:** dealing with God’s righteous opposition to sin --- ## Propitiation and Divine Justice * Christ’s death is not only moral example * It addresses real guilt before a holy Judge * A “legal / forensic” dimension matters * Justification, condemnation, pardon --- ## Redemption * Redemption language frames salvation as liberation * From sin, death, and the powers opposed to God * Ransom imagery is powerful, but must be integrated * It cannot replace justice and guilt categories --- ## Multiple Facets, One Gospel * Classic motifs Craig integrates: * Sacrifice (expiation/propitiation) * Satisfaction of divine justice * Penal substitution * Redemption / victory * Moral influence (real, but not sufficient alone) --- ## Practical Takeaways * Christ’s death is: * Objective (something Christ does for us) * Personal (calls for faith and repentance) * Transformative (new life and holiness) --- ## Navigation * [Return to Christ Overview](Christ.Slides.html) * Prev: [The Incarnation: Two Natures, One Person](Christ.Slides.2.IncarnationTwoNaturesOnePerson.html) * Next: [Christ’s Death: Penal Substitution Defended](Christ.Slides.4.DeathPenalSubstitutionDefended.html) * [Creekside Defenders Main Page](http://tinyurl.com/CreeksideDefenders) ---
