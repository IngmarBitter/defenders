**God's Holiness & Righteousness**

Understanding God's Perfect Goodness


Ingmar Bitter, PhD

--- ## Introduction ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * We've been exploring God's personal attributes. * Previously covered: Intellectual and Volitional Attributes. * Today: Moral Attributes - *Holiness* and *Righteousness*. * Importance: A being worthy of worship must be morally perfect. * Perfect being theology: God is morally perfect by definition. --- ## Key Questions ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * What is the relationship between God and the Good? * What is the content of God's perfect goodness? * Biblical perspective on divine goodness. --- ## Goodness in Scripture ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Biblical terms: Hebrew *t.ôb* / *t.ûb*, English "goodness." * Meanings: * Material blessing * Desirability, pleasantness, beauty * Quality or expense * Moral goodness * Well-being ("the good life") * Biblical focus: God's beneficence or generosity (e.g., Psalms). * Feinberg: God promotes the well-being of His creatures. * Our focus: Moral goodness via *righteousness* and *holiness*. --- ## Righteousness and Holiness ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * **Righteousness** (*s.edeq*, *dikaiosynē theou*): * Dual aspects: Love and Justice. * OT: God as Judge and Ruler, establishing moral order. * Positive (salvific) and negative (punitive) senses. * "God is love" (1 John 4:8 NKJV) + hatred of sin. * **Holiness** (*qōdesh*): * Absolute purity. * Separation from sin. --- ## God's Righteousness: Romans 9:14-21 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

What shall we say then? Is there unrighteousness with God? Certainly not! For He says to Moses, “I will have mercy on whomever I will have mercy, and I will have compassion on whomever I will have compassion.” So then it is not of him who wills, nor of him who runs, but of God who shows mercy. ... Who are you to reply against God? Will the thing formed say to him who formed it, “Why have you made me like this?” Does not the potter have power over the clay...?

Romans 9:14-21 (NKJV)

* God is the ultimate standard of righteousness; no higher appeal. * His mercy and justice are sovereign and unquestionable. * God's will is not subject to human judgment. --- ## God's Presence Sanctifies: Exodus 3:3-5 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Then Moses said, “I will now turn aside and see this great sight, why the bush does not burn.” ... And He said, “Do not draw near this place. Take your sandals off your feet, for the place where you stand is holy ground.”

Exodus 3:3-5 (NKJV)

* God's presence makes a place holy. * Holiness requires reverence and respect. * God is set apart from the ordinary. --- ## God's Call to Holiness: Leviticus 19:2 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Speak to all the congregation of the children of Israel, and say to them: ‘You shall be holy, for I the LORD your God am holy.’

Leviticus 19:2 (NKJV)

* God’s holiness is the basis for our call to holiness. * God sets the moral standard for His people. * Holiness is both God’s nature and His expectation for us. --- ## God's Holiness Praised: Revelation 4:8 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

The four living creatures ... do not rest day or night, saying: “Holy, holy, holy, is the Lord God Almighty, Who was and is and is to come!”

Revelation 4:8 (NKJV)

* God’s holiness is central to heavenly worship. * His holiness is absolute and eternal. * Repetition emphasizes the superlative nature of God’s holiness. --- ## Holiness Exposes Sin: Isaiah 6:1-5 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

I saw the Lord sitting on a throne, high and lifted up, and the train of His robe filled the temple. ... And one [seraph] cried to another and said: “Holy, holy, holy is the LORD of hosts; The whole earth is full of His glory!” ... So I said: “Woe is me, for I am undone! Because I am a man of unclean lips..."

Isaiah 6:1-5 (NKJV)

* God’s holiness reveals human sinfulness. * Encountering God’s holiness leads to humility and repentance. * God’s glory fills all creation. --- ## God Cannot Tolerate Evil: Habakkuk 1:13a ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

You are of purer eyes than to behold evil, And cannot look on wickedness.

Habakkuk 1:13a (NKJV)

* God’s holiness is absolute purity. * He cannot tolerate or approve evil. * God’s nature is fundamentally opposed to wickedness. --- ## Holiness Excludes Sinful Beings from God's Presence ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Rebellious angels were cast out of heaven because of God's holiness. * No one with sin-guilt can remain in God's presence. * Only those cleansed from sin can be with God in heaven. --- ## Rebellious Angels Cast Out: Revelation 12:7-9 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

And war broke out in heaven: Michael and his angels fought with the dragon; and the dragon and his angels fought, but they did not prevail, nor was a place found for them in heaven any longer. So the great dragon was cast out, that serpent of old, called the Devil and Satan, who deceives the whole world; he was cast to the earth, and his angels were cast out with him.

Revelation 12:7-9 (NKJV)

* Even angels are excluded from God's presence when they rebel. * God's holiness enforces separation from evil. * There is no place for sin in heaven. --- ## Only the Holy See God: Hebrews 12:14 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Pursue peace with all people, and holiness, without which no one will see the Lord.

Hebrews 12:14 (NKJV)

* Holiness is required to be in God's presence. * No one can see the Lord without being made holy. * Only Jesus substitutionary death can atone for our sin-guilt. * Once justified, we are called to actively pursue holiness in our lives. --- ## Sin Separates from God: Isaiah 59:1-2 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Behold, the LORD’s hand is not shortened, That it cannot save; Nor His ear heavy, That it cannot hear. But your iniquities have separated you from your God; And your sins have hidden His face from you, So that He will not hear.

Isaiah 59:1-2 (NKJV)

* Sin creates a barrier between people and God. * God’s holiness means He cannot ignore iniquity. * Restoration requires repentance and forgiveness. --- ## Systematic Summary ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * **Euthyphro Dilemma**: * "Does God will it because **it *is* good**", or * "is it good because **God *wills* it**?" * ... ? --- ## Systematic Summary ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * **Euthyphro Dilemma**: * "Does God will it because **it *is* good**", or * "is it good because **God *wills* it**?" * False dilemma: Neither option fits. * Christian response: "God wills something because **He *is* good**." * God’s nature is the standard of goodness. * Moral values (good/evil) rooted in God’s nature. * Moral duties (right/wrong) flow from His commands. --- ## Holiness and Access to God ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * God’s holiness requires absolute purity for anyone in His presence. * No one can see the Lord without being made holy. * Only Jesus’ substitutionary death can remove our sin-guilt and make us holy. * Once justified by Christ, we are called to actively pursue holiness in our lives. --- ## God's Nature vs. God's Will ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * God's nature: Essentially good, loving, just. * God's will: Expresses His nature through commands. * Not arbitrary; aligns with His essence. * Kant: God acts *in accordance with* duty, not *from* duty. * God has no moral duties; ours come from His commands. --- ## Practical Application: Strive for Personal Holiness ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * God calls us to holiness in every area of life. * God hates sin and upholds moral order * (e.g., Moses barred from Promised Land; Ananias and Sapphira). * Holiness brings true happiness: * Seek first God's kingdom and righteousness. --- ## Practical Application: Our hope for Heaven ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Access to God's presence requires true holiness. * No one can see the Lord without being made holy. * Only Jesus' substitutionary death can remove our sin-guilt and make us holy. * After being justified by Christ * we are called to actively pursue holiness in our daily lives. --- ## God Hates Sin: Revelation 14:18-20 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

And another angel ... cried with a loud voice to him who had the sharp sickle, saying, “Thrust in your sharp sickle and gather the clusters of the vine of the earth, for her grapes are fully ripe.” So the angel thrust his sickle into the earth and ... threw them into the great winepress of the wrath of God. And the winepress was trampled ... and blood came out ... as high as the horses’ bridles, for one thousand six hundred furlongs.

Revelation 14:18-20 (NKJV)

* God’s wrath is real and terrifying. * Sin will ultimately be judged. * God’s holiness demands justice. --- ## God Rebukes Sin: Psalm 50:21 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

These things you have done, and I kept silent; You thought that I was altogether like you; But I will rebuke you, And set them in order before your eyes.

Psalm 50:21 (NKJV)

* God is not indifferent to sin. * His silence is not approval. * God will ultimately confront and correct wrongdoing. --- ## Called to Holiness: 1 Peter 1:14-16 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

As obedient children, not conforming yourselves to the former lusts, as in your ignorance; but as He who called you is holy, you also be holy in all your conduct, because it is written, “Be holy, for I am holy.”

1 Peter 1:14-16 (NKJV)

* God’s holiness is our model for living. * We are called to leave behind former sinful ways. * Holiness should characterize all our conduct. --- ## Holiness Brings Happiness: Matthew 6:33 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

But seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things shall be added to you.

Matthew 6:33 (NKJV)

* Prioritize God’s kingdom and righteousness above all. * God provides for those who pursue holiness. * True fulfillment comes from seeking God first. --- ## The Gospel Reveals God's Righteousness: Romans 1:16-17 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ, for it is the power of God to salvation for everyone who believes ... For in it the righteousness of God is revealed from faith to faith; as it is written, “The just shall live by faith.”

Romans 1:16-17 (NKJV)

* The gospel reveals God’s righteousness. * Salvation is available to everyone who believes. * Righteousness comes by faith, not by works. --- ## Righteousness Through Faith: Romans 3:21-26 ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

But now the righteousness of God apart from the law is revealed ... through faith in Jesus Christ, to all ... who believe. For there is no difference; for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus, whom God set forth as a propitiation by His blood, through faith, to demonstrate His righteousness...

Romans 3:21-26 (NKJV)

* All have sinned and need God’s grace. * Justification is a free gift through faith in Christ. * Christ’s sacrifice demonstrates God’s righteousness and mercy. --- ## Conclusion ![](OmniPics/GodHoly.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * God’s moral attributes: *Holiness* and *Righteousness*. * God’s nature defines goodness; His commands set our duties. * Application: Pursue holiness, trust Christ’s righteousness. * Reflection: *How does God’s holiness shape your daily life?* Version 2025.05.10 --- [ingmarbitter.github.io/defenders/RevelationAndFoundations/GodAttributes/GodImmutability.slides.html](https://ingmarbitter.github.io/defenders/RevelationAndFoundations/GodAttributes/GodImmutability.slides.html) [TinyUrl.com/CreeksideDefenders](../CreeksideDefenders.html) [creeksidechristian.churchcenter.com/groups/join/CSvr6Z1Aj2MptxFEhv7rKDe6](https://creeksidechristian.churchcenter.com/groups/join/CSvr6Z1Aj2MptxFEhv7rKDe6)
