**God's Omnipotence**

Exploring the Attribute of God's Power


Ingmar Bitter, PhD

--- ## Omnipotence Word Meaning ![](OmniPics/BrainPower.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * From Latin: *omni* (all) + *potentia* (power) * God is all-powerful * Possesses unlimited power * Able to do all things * Infinite in capability * A volitional (by His will) attribute of God --- ## God as Almighty (Genesis 17:1) ![](OmniPics/GodAbraham.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

When Abram was ninety-nine years old, the LORD appeared to Abram and said to him, “I am **Almighty God**; walk before Me and be blameless.”

Genesis 17:1 (NKJV)

* God reveals Himself as "El Shaddai" (God Almighty) to Abram * Establishes His supreme power and authority --- ## God as Almighty (Revelation 19:6) ![](OmniPics/GodOmnipotent.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

And I heard, as it were, the voice of a great multitude, as the sound of many waters and as the sound of mighty thunderings, saying, “Alleluia! For **the Lord God Omnipotent** reigns!”

Revelation 19:6 (NKJV)

* Affirms God's almighty power in the NT * His reign is characterized by omnipotence * **Pantokratōr**: Greek, all-ruling, almighty, supreme authority * **Autarkēs**: Greek, self-sufficient * **El Shaddai**: Hebrew, meaning of both, Pantokratōr and Autarkēs * NKJV appropriately uses two different English words --- ## Creation as a Display of Power (Genesis 1:1) ![](OmniPics/GodCreation.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.

Genesis 1:1 (NKJV)

* Creation ex nihilo (out of nothing) showcases God's unlimited power * He creates matter, energy, and all existence by His word --- ## Creation as a Display of Power (Psalm 33:9) ![](OmniPics/GodWordEarth.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

For He spoke, and it was done; He commanded, and it stood fast.

Psalm 33:9 (NKJV)

* God's word alone brings the universe into being * Highlights the effortless nature of His creative power --- ## Creation and Resurrection (Romans 4:17) ![](OmniPics/GodIntoExistence.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

[...] God, who gives life to the dead and calls those things which do not exist as though they did;

Romans 4:17b (NKJV)

* He "summons the things that do not exist into existence" * "Kalountos" (καλοῦντος): calling, summoning, bringing into existence * "Ta mē onta" (τὰ μὴ ὄντα): things that are not, nonexistent things * "Hōs onta" (ὡς ὄντα): as being, as though they are * Demonstrates God's power in creation and resurrection --- ## Nothing Too Hard for God (Genesis 18:14) ![](OmniPics/GodAllPossible.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Is anything too hard for the LORD? At the appointed time I will return to you, according to the time of life, and Sarah shall have a son.

Genesis 18:14 (NKJV)

* Rhetorical question affirms nothing is beyond God's power * His promise to Abraham and Sarah shows He can do the impossible --- ## Nothing Too Hard for God (Jeremiah 32:17) ![](OmniPics/GodOutstretchedArms.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Ah, Lord GOD! Behold, You have made the heavens and the earth by Your great power and outstretched arm. There is nothing too hard for You.

Jeremiah 32:17 (NKJV)

* Affirms that nothing is too difficult for God * His power in creation underscores His omnipotence --- ## Nothing Too Hard for God (Jeremiah 32:26-27) ![](OmniPics/GodUmlimitedPower.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Then the word of the LORD came to Jeremiah, saying, “Behold, I am the LORD, the God of all flesh. Is there anything too hard for Me?”

Jeremiah 32:26-27 (NKJV)

* God's rhetorical question reinforces His limitless power * As God of all flesh, His authority is universal --- ## God Can Do All Things (Job 42:1-2) ![](OmniPics/GodCanDoAll.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Then Job answered the LORD and said: “I know that You can do everything, And that no purpose of Yours can be withheld from You.”

Job 42:1-2 (NKJV)

* Job acknowledges God's ability to do all things * His purposes cannot be thwarted by any limitation --- ## God Can Do All Things (Matthew 19:26) ![](OmniPics/JesusAllPossible.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

But Jesus looked at them and said to them, “With men this is impossible, but with God all things are possible.”

Matthew 19:26 (NKJV)

* Jesus affirms God's limitless power * Contrasts human inability with divine capability --- ## God Can Do All Things (Mark 14:36) ![](OmniPics/GodJesus.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

And He said, “Abba, Father, all things are possible for You. Take this cup away from Me; nevertheless, not what I will, but what You will.”

Mark 14:36 (NKJV)

* Jesus acknowledges God's ability to do all things * Submits to the Father's will despite His power --- ## Systematic Summary: Omnipotence ![](OmniPics/GodSpheresOfPower.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * **Omnipotence**: God has unlimited power and can bring about any state of affairs logically possible for anyone in that situation * **Power**: His degree of power is maximal, cannot be exceeded * An omnipotent being finds all actions easy, regardless of difficulty * **Range**: The range of logically possible actions * Excludes logical impossibilities (e.g., creating a square circle) * Such restrictions do not diminish omnipotence, as they are not due to a lack of power * Same range but weaker power is no longer omnipotent --- ## Paradoxes of Omnipotence: Acting Contrary to Nature ![](OmniPics/EscherStrairsImpossible.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Can God act against His own nature (e.g., sin or create another god and worship him)? * No, such actions are inconsistent with His essential properties * Omnipotence does not include violating His own nature --- ## Can God Sin? ![](OmniPics/GodSin.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

This is the message which we have heard from Him and declare to you, that God is light and in Him is no darkness at all.

1 John 1:5 (NKJV)

* God's omnipotence does not include the ability to sin * Sinning is contrary to His holy and loving nature * Scripture affirms God's perfect goodness: * "God cannot be tempted by evil" (James 1:13) * "Your eyes are too pure to look on evil" (Habakkuk 1:13) * "The Lord is good" (Psalm 100:5) * God's inability to sin reflects His perfect nature, not a limitation --- ## The Reality of Jesus' Temptation ![](OmniPics/JesusTemptation.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

But He answered and said, “It is written, ‘Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceeds from the mouth of God.’”

Matthew 4:4 (NKJV)

* Jesus faced genuine temptation in His humanity (Matthew 4:1-11, Luke 4:1-13) * Satan's temptations exploited His physical needs, e.g. hunger * Not morally wrong, but still contrary to God's planned provision * Yet, Jesus relied on Scripture, resisted a suffering-free choice * But He experienced the real pull of Satan's offers --- ## Jesus and the Possibility of Sin ![](OmniPics/JesusSinLess.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

For we do not have a High Priest who cannot sympathize with our weaknesses, but was in all points tempted as we are, yet without sin.

Hebrews 4:15 (NKJV)

* Jesus, truly God and truly human, was genuinely tempted * His divine nature, including holiness, ensured He could not sin * Additional scriptures: * "He committed no sin" (1 Peter 2:22) * "God made him who had no sin" (2 Corinthians 5:21) * Jesus' sinlessness, rooted in His divine nature, makes Him the perfect substitute for us --- ## Paradoxes of Omnipotence: Logical Impossibilities ![](OmniPics/EscherWaterfallHouse.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Can God do logically impossible things? * Create a square circle? * Create an immovable object and an irresistible force at the same time and place? * No, logical impossibilities are contradictions, not "things" * God's power does not extend to logical absurdities --- ## Paradoxes of Omnipotence: Unactualizable Worlds ![](InfinityPics/PossibleWorlds.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Can God create any logically possible world (e.g., a sinless world with free creatures)? * Not necessarily, due to creaturely freedom and would-truths vs could-truths * Some worlds may be infeasible despite being logically possible * Sometimes people who **could** choose to do good **would** not do so --- ## Resolution of Paradoxes ![](OmniPics/EscherPortait.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Limitations on God's power are purely logical, not due to lack of power * Logic reflects God's nature; He cannot act inconsistently with Himself * Omnipotence does not require doing the logically impossible --- ## Definition of Omnipotence ![](OmniPics/GodOptions.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * **Power**: * **There is no state of affairs that God is unable to bring about due to a lack of power** * His power is unlimited and cannot be exceeded by any other being * **Range**: * **God can bring about any state of affairs which is logically possible for anyone to bring about in that situation** * Excludes: * Acting against His nature, Logical contradictions * Infeasible worlds due to creaturely freedom --- ## Key Takeaways on Omnipotence ![](OmniPics/GodSpheres.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * God is almighty and can do all things consistent with His nature and logic * His power is displayed in creation, providence, and redemption * We trust in His sovereign power and find security in His ability * Worship and awe flow from His unmatched omnipotence --- ## Practical Applications of Omnipotence ![](OmniPics/GodMeetsNeeds.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Understanding omnipotence is not just theoretical; it changes how we live * God’s power applies directly to our daily struggles and purpose * Three applications 1. God's power works in us 2. Nothing can defeat God's purposes 3. God is adequate to meet all our needs --- ## Application 1: God's Power Works In Us ![](OmniPics/GodPowerInUs.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

For it is the God who commanded light to shine out of darkness, who has shone in our hearts to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ. But we have this treasure in earthen vessels, that the excellence of the power may be of God and not of us. We are hard-pressed on every side, yet not crushed; we are perplexed, but not in despair; persecuted, but not forsaken; struck down, but not destroyed—always carrying about in the body the dying of the Lord Jesus, that the life of Jesus also may be manifested in our body.

2 Corinthians 4:6-10 (NKJV)

* The same power that created the universe is at work in us * This power isn’t ours—it’s God’s, working through fragile humans * God's power sustains us through affliction, persecution, and trials * His life is manifested in our mortal bodies --- ## God's Power In Us (Ephesians 1:19-21) ![](OmniPics/GodTouchingMan.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

...and what is the exceeding greatness of His power toward us who believe, according to the working of His mighty power which He worked in Christ when He raised Him from the dead and seated Him at His right hand in the heavenly places, far above all principality and power and might and dominion, and every name that is named, not only in this age but also in that which is to come.

Ephesians 1:19-21 (NKJV)

* The power that raised Jesus from the dead is at work in us. * This power is far above all rule, authority, and dominion. --- ## God's Power Beyond Our Imagination (Ephesians 3:20-21) ![](OmniPics/GobPowerBeyond.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Now to Him who is able to do exceedingly abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that works in us, to Him be glory in the church by Christ Jesus to all generations, forever and ever. Amen.

Ephesians 3:20-21 (NKJV)

* God's power exceeds our limited vision * We must ask and think for greater things, trusting in His ability --- ## The Vine and the Branches (John 15:5) ![](OmniPics/Vine.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

I am the vine, you are the branches. He who abides in Me, and I in him, bears much fruit; for without Me you can do nothing.

John 15:5 (NKJV)

* Apart from Christ, we can do nothing of spiritual significance * Abiding in Him is the key to bearing fruit * Therefore: * Apart from Christ, we can do much less than we think * Through Christ, we can do much more than we think --- ## Strength Through Christ (Philippians 4:13) ![](OmniPics/StrengthThroughChrist.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me.

Philippians 4:13 (NKJV)

* His strength enables us to accomplish God's will * Again: * Apart from Christ, we can do much less than we think * Through Christ, we can do much more than we think --- ## God's Power in Weakness (2 Corinthians 12:8-10) ![](OmniPics/ChickenPower.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

Concerning this thing I pleaded with the Lord three times that it might depart from me. And He said to me, “My grace is sufficient for you, for My strength is made perfect in weakness.” Therefore most gladly I will rather boast in my infirmities, that the power of Christ may rest upon me. Therefore I take pleasure in infirmities, in reproaches, in needs, in persecutions, in distresses, for Christ’s sake. For when I am weak, then I am strong.

2 Corinthians 12:8-10 (NKJV)

* God's power is most evident in our weakness * Paul rejoices in his weaknesses because they magnify God's strength * God doesn’t just overcome our weakness—He shines through it * Our limitations become platforms for His strength --- ## Example: Fred and Elaine ![](OmniPics/BibleStudy.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Fred was serving in church and his communite * Fred’s stroke brought him to a nursing home * He and Elaine started a Bible study in the nursing home to encourage others in despair * God’s power turned their frailty into a means to reach the unreached --- ## Application 2: Nothing Can Defeat God's Purposes ![](OmniPics/Decree.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")

In him, according to the purpose of him who accomplishes all things according to the counsel of his will...

Ephesians 1:11 (NKJV)

* God's purposes will always be accomplished * No force — human, natural, or spiritual — can derail God’s plans * His omnipotence guarantees His purposes prevail * This gives us confidence in chaos and setbacks --- ## Joseph: A Case Study in Providence ![](OmniPics/StringsAndScroll.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Joseph's life illustrates God's providential purposes: * Betrayed by his brothers * Falsely accused by Potiphar's wife * Imprisoned unjustly * Yet, God used these events to save Joseph's family and fulfill His plan for Israel * As we trust in him, walk with him, and rely upon his power, we can have confidence that God’s purposes for our lives will be accomplished --- ## God's Will May Include Failure ![](OmniPics/GodInGoodAndSuffering.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * God's will for our lives may include failure and suffering * Failure teaches lessons that success cannot * Examples: * Persecution and martyrdom * Loss of jobs or positions * But not all failure is a lesson from God * Consequence of the fall (sickness) * Free will (evil deeds of others) * Either way, there is no guarantee for success in this life --- ## The Health and Wealth Gospel Lie ![](OmniPics/JesusFerrari.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * The "health and wealth gospel" falsely promises worldly success for believers * True biblical teaching acknowledges that God's will may include suffering and hardship * God's purposes are spiritual, not necessarily material * Some examples of material blessings do not make it a rule --- ## Application 3: God Is Adequate to All Our Needs ![](OmniPics/GodAndPrayer.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * God's omnipotence means He is sufficient for every need: * No prayer is too hard * No need is too great * No temptation is too strong * No misery is too deep --- ## Trusting and Worshiping God's Omnipotence ![](OmniPics/GodTrust.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * **Trusting God's Omnipotence**: * Even in failure and suffering, His will prevails * He works all things for the good of those who love Him (Romans 8:28). * **Worshiping an Omnipotent God**: * God's omnipotence inspires worship and awe. * We glorify Him for His unmatched power and sovereignty. * To Him be glory forever and ever (Ephesians 3:21). --- ## Closing: Power for Today, Hope for Tomorrow ![](OmniPics/LookingUpInWorship.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem")
