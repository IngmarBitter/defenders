**The Ontological Argument**

Natural Theology

If God could exist, He must exist

--- ## What Is the Ontological Argument? ![](NatTheoPics/Spiral1.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * A deductive argument for God from the very concept of God * Classic origin: Anselm of Canterbury (Proslogion, 1078) * Core idea: * God := the greatest conceivable being * "that than which nothing greater can be conceived" * If such a being is even possible, then God must exist * https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xBmAKCvWl74 --- ## Anselm’s Insight and Scripture ![](NatTheoPics/Pyramids.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Definition (Latin): aliquid quo nihil maius cogitari potest * If the greatest conceivable being did not exist, it would not be greatest * Therefore, once understood, God’s existence follows * Psalm 14:1 (NKJV): * “The fool has said in his heart, ‘There is no God.’” --- ## Possible Worlds Primer ![](NatTheoPics/PossibleWorlds.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Possible world = a maximal, consistent way reality could be * A complete set of propositions describing reality * Metaphysical vs. epistemic possibility: * Epistemic: for all I know, maybe true/maybe false * Metaphysical: what can really be (actualizable), independent of our knowledge --- ## Plantinga’s Modal Version ![](NatTheoPics/Logic.jpg style="float: right; width: 8rem") * Maximal excellence := * omnipotent, omniscient, morally perfect * Maximal greatness := * maximal excellence in every possible world * The argument: 1. It's possible that a maximally great being exists 2. If possibly exists, then exists in some possible world 3. If in some world, then in every possible world 4. If in every possible world, then in the actual world 5. If in the actual world, then exists 6. Therefore, a maximally great being exists --- ## Why Premise (1) Matters ![](NatTheoPics/Spiral2.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * The hinge: Is a maximally great being metaphysically possible? * Not epistemic “maybe,” but real possibility * Coherence test: * “Married bachelor” is incoherent; MG being is not * No hidden contradiction in omnipotence, omniscience, moral perfection across worlds --- ## Addressing Parodies (Islands, Lions, Pizzas) ![](NatTheoPics/Spiral3.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * “Most-perfect island” fails: * No intrinsic maxima (always add more palm trees, etc.) * No objective great-making properties for islands * “Necessary lion/pizza” fails: * Concrete, material kinds can’t exist in all possible worlds (e.g., singularity-only worlds) * Necessity here is incoherent for such entities --- ## The Quasi-Maximally Great Being Challenge ![](NatTheoPics/Spiral4.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Claim: a being like God but slightly deficient (e.g., not omniscient) * Response: * Maximal greatness includes omnipotence; can refrain from creating * There are worlds with only the MG being * Then quasi-MG beings cannot exist in every world * Maximal and quasi-maximal greatness are logically incompatible --- ## A Posteriori Support: The Value Argument ![](NatTheoPics/ScaleGoodArgument.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Joshua Rasmussen’s “Value Argument”: * Some degree of value is instantiated * If some degree is instantiable, each degree is instantiable * Maximal greatness is a degree of value * Therefore, maximal greatness can be instantiated * Supports the plausibility of premise (1) --- ## Cumulative Case Support ![](NatTheoPics/Arguments.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Other arguments suggest a necessary, perfect mind: * Contingency: metaphysically necessary source * Moral: paradigm of moral value and duty * Mathematics: omniscient, necessary mind grounding applicability * Simplicity: one necessary, omnipotent, omniscient, morally perfect being --- ## Chain Mail, Not Chain Links ![](NatTheoPics/ChainMail.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * Natural theology works cumulatively * Ontological argument joins other arguments * Together they reinforce a robust theistic conclusion --- ## Summary and Conclusion ![](NatTheoPics/Spiral5.jpg style="float: right; width: 9rem") * If a maximally great being is possible, God exists * The concept appears coherent and supported by value and cumulative considerations * Conclusion: A maximally great being exists --- Version 2025.08.02
